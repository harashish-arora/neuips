\section{Data curation}
\label{sec:data_curation}

\scthree{} is derived from \textsc{BigSolDB}~v2.1~\citep{krasnov2025bigsoldb}:
\numval{112\,465} mole-fraction solubility measurements spanning
\numval{1\,525} solutes, \numval{218} solvents, \numval{1\,687} literature
sources, and \numval{243.15}--\numval{425.77}~K.
An initial pass revealed \numval{9} DOIs with systematic errors (§\ref{sec:waterfall}), \numval{46} DOIs in which data was repeated from other sources (§\ref{sec:raw_audit}), and transcription mistakes between papers (§\ref{sec:sergei}).
Such findings motivated a pipeline consisting of three phases: a \emph{raw
audit} phase (§\ref{sec:raw_audit}), a \emph{canonicalization} phase (§\ref{sec:canonicalization}), and a \emph{cleaning waterfall} phase (§\ref{sec:waterfall}),
which reduced the raw table to \numval{101\,535} measurements. It is on this dataset that
we perform our calculations for the aleatoric limit (§\ref{sec:aleatoric}) and benchmark
splits (§\ref{sec:benchmark}).

\subsection{Raw-data audit}
\label{sec:raw_audit}

Our first phase in the pipeline is to inspect the raw \textsc{BigSolDB} dataset (which in addition to the solubility dataset, also provides solvent density data for solvents as a supplementary dataset). Each row carries solute and solvent identifiers (\texttt{SMILES\_Solute}, \texttt{SMILES\_Solvent}), temperature,
mole fraction $x$, and a reported $\log_{10} S$ (mol/L) (which is either derived
from $x$ and solvent density, or is reporter directly in the literature).
Apart from one invalid solute \SMILES{} (which is parsed as a polymer and subsequently ignored, see \ref{sec:waterfall}), all \numval{1\,525} solute \SMILES{} pass \texttt{RDKit} (a cheminformatics library for parsing and manipulating \SMILES{}).

\paragraph{Internal consistency of {\logS}.}
\numval{3\,187} rows (\numval{2.8\,\%}) carry \texttt{NaN} in the reported
\logS{} column. For the \numval{109\,278} rows with finite mole fraction, temperature, and a non-missing reported $\log_{10} S$, we reconstruct $\log_{10} S$ from the
mole fraction $x$ using the standard relation between mole fraction and
molar solubility.  We fit the provided solvent-density data per solvent to a linear law $\rho(T) = aT + b$ (g\,cm$^{-3}$), and
evaluate $\rho$ at each row's temperature; solvent molar mass $M_w$ is
obtained from the solvent \SMILES{} via \texttt{RDKit}.  Specifically,
we evaluate:
\[
    \log_{10} S = \log_{10}\!\left(\frac{x}{1-x}\cdot\frac{\rho(T)\,\cdot\,10^{3}}{M_w}\right),
\]
and we compare the result to the reported $\log_{10} S$.
Residuals are negligible (median $|\mathrm{residual}| = 9.9\times 10^{-6}$,
$P_{99} = 1.25\times 10^{-3}$); only \numval{8} rows exceed $0.01$~log~S,
all from a single DOI (\verb|10.1016/j.molliq.2017.02.075|, $\beta$-alanine
in methanol), which we add to the bad-DOI list (§\ref{sec:waterfall}).
This agreement is what licenses us to use the \emph{same} closed form in
the cleaning waterfall to compute $\log_{10} S$ for the rows in which
{\logS} is \texttt{NaN} (§\ref{sec:waterfall}, step~W5), wherein only \numval{14} rows are dropped because no density route returns a positive $\rho$ for that solvent at that temperature.

\paragraph{Cross-DOI bit-exact matches.}
We group rows by (canonical solute, canonical solvent, $T$ rounded to
$0.1$~K, $\log_{10} S$ rounded to four decimals): there are \numval{86} such four-tuples
reported by two or more DOIs at identical values, which affects \numval{172} rows
across \numval{46} DOIs --- this is an artifact of data duplication between sources, and is consumed downstream when we build independence groups for the aleatoric analysis (§\ref{sec:source}).

\subsection{Canonicalization}
\label{sec:canonicalization}

We canonicalize all solute and solvent \SMILES{} with \texttt{RDKit},
preserving stereochemistry (chirality and $E/Z$ geometry) and we explicitly choose to \emph{not} enumerate tautomers; this leaves all \numval{1\,525} raw solute \SMILES{} distinct. Alternative policies that strip stereochemistry or enumerate tautomers were considered and rejected, because the merges they induce conflate compounds whose measured solubilities disagree by $5$--$20\times$ the aleatoric floor. The candidate policies, an empirical audit of those merges, and the chemical interpretation are deferred to Appendix~\ref{app:canonicalization}.

\subsection{Manual corrections}
\label{sec:sergei}

The source-integrity analysis (§\ref{sec:source}) flagged \numval{22} DOIs
with mean inter-lab deviation $\geq 0.6$~log~S against peer consensus.
Manual review of those papers identifies four transcription bugs that we
correct in place (Table~\ref{tab:sergei}), recovering \numval{58} rows
that the bad-DOI filter (W1) would otherwise drop wholesale.

\paragraph{W3 --- salts and mixtures.}  The \numval{10\,042} dropped
rows correspond to \numval{193} distinct multi-component solute
\SMILES{}.  A single $\log_{10} S$ label is not directly comparable
across salt and neutral entries because their dissolution thermodynamics
differ structurally; we exclude them here and leave a salt-aware split
for future work~\todo{FIND CITATION}.

\paragraph{W5 --- $\log_{10} S$ recovery.}  We re-use the same closed
form as in §\ref{sec:raw_audit}, resolving $\rho$ at the row's
$(\text{solvent}, T)$ from one of three sources: (i)~the experimental value from
\textsc{BigSolDB}~v2.1's solvent-density table when present; (ii)~else
the $(a,b)$ linear-fit coefficients from §\ref{sec:raw_audit}; (iii)~else the
\texttt{thermo} library (a thermophysical property library for estimating density from temperature and pressure).  Of the
\numval{2\,631} rows that reach step W5 with \texttt{NaN} \logS{} (§\ref{sec:waterfall}, step W5),
\numval{2\,617} recover a finite \logS{}; the remaining \numval{14} cannot be assigned
a positive $\rho$ by any tier and are dropped.

\paragraph{W7 --- intra-DOI dedupe.}  All \numval{40} removed rows come
from a single DOI (\texttt{10.1016/j.jct.2020.106137}) that reports
each nominal temperature twice ($T = x.15$ and $x.16$~K) with identical
\logS{} values, which we attribute to a rounding artifact.

\paragraph{Final cleaned dataset.}
After canonicalization (Option D), the four manual corrections, and the seven-stage waterfall above, \scthree{} (Table~\ref{tab:final_cleaned}) contains \numval{101\,535} measurements covering \numval{1\,327} canonical solutes, \numval{206} solvents, and \numval{1\,493} DOIs, with \numval{746} $(\text{solute}, \text{solvent})$ pairs measured by two or more independent DOIs (the multi-source pool that the aleatoric analysis of §\ref{sec:aleatoric} consumes).
